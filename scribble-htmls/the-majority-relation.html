The Majority Relation

Brett Schreiber

Consider the list '(a b a c a). Here, 'a is the majority element. Now consider the list '(x x y z). Although x is the most frequent element, it is not the majority. Notice that a list can have zero or one majority elements.

Let’s define a relation majorityo in miniKanren. It takes an element e and a list l and asserts that e is the majority element in l. For example, the following miniKanren statement should succeed. (majorityo 'x '(a x b x a x x c x)). I always like to start by drawing a picture and adding annotations.

A few takeaways:

  • l can be described as a disjoint union, or riffle, of two lists l₁ and l₂.

  • l₁ is longer than l₂, that is, it has a greater length.

  • l₁ consists entirely of es. It is e, repeated.

Writing this out in miniKanren:

(defrel (majorityo e l)
  (fresh (l₁ l₂ l₁⊔l₂)
         (== l l₁⊔l₂)
         (repetitiono e l₁)
         (longero l₁ l₂)
         (riffleo l₁ l₂ l₁⊔l₂)))
